# Innovation Framing

## Inputs
- User-supplied innovation, if any
- Topic keywords and constraints
- Literature search results

## Output Standard
Each candidate framing must include:
- A 1-sentence claim
- Why the claim matters
- What existing work already covers
- What the gap is
- What evidence would be needed
- What would falsify the claim

## Rejection Rules
Reject framings that:
- Only restate a popular direction without a concrete gap
- Depend on unavailable data/compute/human annotation with no fallback
- Require fabricated or unverifiable evidence
- Collapse into a survey when the user asked for an empirical paper

## Preferred Candidate Style
- Narrow enough to test
- Clear reviewer-facing motivation
- Evidence path known in advance
- Honest about risk and negative-result outcomes
