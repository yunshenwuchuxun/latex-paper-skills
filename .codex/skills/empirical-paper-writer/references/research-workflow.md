# Empirical Research Workflow

## Objective
Turn a fixed topic and a candidate contribution into an evidence-first paper plan.

## Phase 0: Research Snapshot
- Identify the closest prior papers, baselines, and standard datasets/metrics.
- Keep the initial set compact: enough to frame the problem and likely reviewer comparison set.
- Reject papers that are unrelated, unverifiable, or too far from the main claim.

## Phase 1: Contribution Framing
- Define the primary claim and 1-2 secondary claims.
- State what the paper does **not** claim.
- Identify the minimum evidence needed for each claim.

## Phase 2: Experiment Contract
- Lock datasets, baselines, metrics, ablations, and analysis requirements.
- Decide which results are `planned`, `placeholder`, or `verified`.
- Tie every experiment to a claim.

## Phase 3: Draft Preparation
- Build the outline and section skeleton.
- Insert figure/table placeholders only when they correspond to planned or verified evidence.
- Do not write deterministic result conclusions before evidence exists.
