## Confirmed Outline (edit to match the user-approved outline)
1. Introduction
   - Motivation: adapt LLMs under compute/memory constraints
   - Why PEFT now (LLM scale + multi-task personalization)
   - Contributions: taxonomy + systems view + eval checklist
2. Scope, Definitions, and Efficiency Metrics
   - PEFT definition vs full fine-tuning / in-context learning
   - Efficiency metrics: trainable params, optimizer memory, throughput, latency
   - Problem settings: SFT / instruction tuning / domain adaptation / multi-task
3. Taxonomy of PEFT Methods
   - Additive modules: (serial/parallel) adapters, side-tuning, routing
   - Reparameterization: low-rank updates (LoRA family) and variants
   - Prompt-based: prompt/prefix/P-tuning; (soft) prompts as parameters
   - Selective tuning: bias-only, IA$^3$, partial layer tuning, masks/sparsity
4. Design Space and Method Composition (2022--2026)
   - Rank allocation, layer selection, initialization, regularization
   - Multi-adapter composition: stacking, merging, task arithmetic, routing
   - Continual / personalization settings; interference and forgetting
5. Systems View: Training and Serving Efficiency
   - Memory breakdown (weights/activations/optimizer states) and bottlenecks
   - Quantized PEFT: 8-bit/4-bit finetuning (e.g., QLoRA-style pipelines)
   - Serving: mergeable adapters, hot-swapping, checkpoint formats, tooling
6. Evaluation and Benchmarks
   - Benchmark coverage: general, instruction-following, domain, multilingual
   - Reporting checklist: cost metrics + hyperparams + reproducibility
   - Failure modes: robustness, out-of-distribution, safety regressions
7. Applications and Case Studies
   - Open-source LLM instruction tuning recipes using PEFT
   - Domain specialization (code, medical, legal, finance)
   - Multi-modal / vision-language PEFT (optional, depending on scope)
8. Safety, Governance, and Open Challenges
   - Safety risks of low-cost adaptation; alignment drift
   - Data/privacy + provenance considerations for adapter sharing
   - Open problems and future directions; conclusion
